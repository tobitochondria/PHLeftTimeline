## About

The Philippine Left Timeline is a free, open-access single-page web application that documents the formation, evolution, and dissolution of leftist organizations in the Philippines. It is intended as an educational and research reference for students, academics, journalists, and the general public.

Built and maintained by **Toby Giongco**.

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

<details>
<summary><strong>What is the Philippine Left Timeline?</strong></summary>

The Philippine Left Timeline is a free, open-access interactive timeline that chronicles the formation, evolution, and dissolution of leftist organizations in the Philippines — from the first labor unions of the American colonial period in 1901 to the pluralized left of today. It is intended as an educational and research reference for students, academics, journalists, and the general public.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>What is its scope?</strong></summary>

The timeline currently focuses on the formation of organizations — political parties, mass movements, armed groups, labor federations, youth and student organizations, peasant federations, cultural formations, and women's groups that constitute the organizational history of the Philippine Left. Future updates will expand coverage to include critical nationwide events the Left participated in or was responsible for.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Will data be updated in the future?</strong></summary>

Yes. This is an ongoing project. New entries, corrections to existing records, and expanded event coverage will be added over time.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>What are your sources?</strong></summary>

Data is drawn from public sources including books, Wikipedia, archival web material, and official organization websites. A future update will add in-line source citations inside each individual event card on the timeline.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>What do you mean by "the Left"?</strong></summary>

The term broadly refers to political parties, organizations, mass movements, and armed groups that self-identify or are historically classified as socialist, communist, nationalist-democratic, or progressive in orientation. This includes formations across the Marxist-Leninist, social-democratic, and national-democratic traditions, as well as labor and peasant federations that have historically aligned with the left.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Why does the timeline begin in 1901?</strong></summary>

1901 marks the formal beginning of American colonial rule in the Philippines following the Philippine-American War. This period saw the emergence of early labor unions, nationalist organizations, and proto-leftist formations — making it the most logical starting point for tracing the roots of organized left politics in the country.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>What kinds of organizations are included?</strong></summary>

Political parties, militant mass organizations, armed revolutionary groups, labor unions, peasant federations, youth and student alliances, women's organizations, cultural institutions, and broad coalitions — in short, any formation that constitutes a meaningful part of the organizational landscape of the Philippine Left.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Is this project affiliated with any political party or organization?</strong></summary>

No. This is an independent historical reference project with no affiliation to any political party, group, or institution. It is intended as a neutral, educational resource and does not advocate for or against any political tendency.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Can I use this for academic or research purposes?</strong></summary>

Yes, with appropriate attribution. Always verify information against primary sources for formal academic work, as this is a reference resource and not a peer-reviewed publication.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>How can I report an error or suggest an addition?</strong></summary>

Reach out via [email](mailto:tobitochondria@gmail.com) or open an issue on [GitHub](https://github.com/tobitochondria/PHLeftTimeline). All corrections and suggestions for new entries are very welcome.

</details>
